Links: What are they?

Links are a way to take the user from the current website, to either a different website or even another part of the same website.

How Do They Work?

Links use the tags "a" and require the attribute href, followed by = and the website URL, in order to tell the page where it is taking you. The text that follows the URL is what the user will see highlighted and what they click.

Types of Links

These are the types of link we will cover

    External link (another website) Example: freeCodeCamp
    Internal link (your own file) Example: Go to Lesson 4
    Open link in new tab (Optional but useful) Example: Google

Important Things to Remember

    Do not forget https:// (www. will not be enough)
    Mispelling href
    Leaving the link text empty
    Linking to a file that does not exist
